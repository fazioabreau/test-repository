Yoga Mahila where balance unites mind, body and spirit.

The practice of Yoga has existed for a really, really, really long time.
Some say, 10,000 years, but they weren't around then, so who really knows.
However, what we do know according to two of the most notable
Yoga gurus of OUR time is:

Yoga is the uniting of mind, body and spirit,
through the practice of physical postures (asanas)
combined with disciplined breathing patterns (pranayama)
drawing the practitioner into a state of wellness and bliss. B.K.S. Iyengar,
is most notably known for alignment and therapy.
K. Pattabhi Jois is most notably known for posture and breath control.

When we think of life — we sometimes think after a certain age
it all stops and now what?
Think about the times you have decided not to begin something new,
maybe it's a new career, returning to school, a new hobby, etc . . .
Why? Because we live in an ageist society drilling in our brain “you’re too old.”
I'm here to tell you it's not so; in watching Tao Porchon-Lynch
I’ve learned that life begins when you believe in yourself and
realize you CAN do anything at any age.
Tao has shown us to be positive, because what you think occurs.
When Tao had hip and wrist surgery she was told by doctors
she wouldn’t be able to do advanced Yoga again,
to which she replied, “I’m not interested in what I can’t do only in what I can.”
Now that's a way to approach life.
